1950- There was 1200 lbs of litter per day alone in Times Square in 1950. A giant grbage can was put in the middle of Town Square to prevent littering. The garbage can was there to remind residents to dispose of their garbage properly.



1968- In New York City 1968, The Sanitation Department went on strike. This is known as the "Great Garbage Strike of 1968". The Sanitaion Department refused to pick up and garbage of litter for nine days. This occured because of poor working conditions and low pay.



1970- On April 22, Earth Day was founded by U.S Senator Gaylord Nelson after witnessing an oil spill in Santa Barbra, California. He was inpired by a student and realized he could inform others on the effects of air and water pollution. He announced it as "national teach-in on the environment". On April 22, 1970 20 million citizens cleaned steets, parks etc to demonstrate a healthy and cleaned environment.



1972-The Clean Water Act and the Marine Protection, Research, and Sanctuaries Act were passed the same year to limit the amount of goods being tossed in the oceans. U.S. politicians passed these because at this time, they realized how severe the issue was. Yet, this has gone on for a long time around the world because businesses produced goods at such rapid rates. Inside these goods, they contained toxic chemicals that were soon consumed by marine life. Over time and through biomagnification, these toxins were consumed by humans. These acts significantly reduced the amount of litter going into the oceans in America.
